Voice in plain english

Shamus writes like a 20-year-old who made it and refuses to pretend otherwise. Every paragraph earns its place with a named person, a dollar number, or a geographic pin — and every big win gets attribution-flipped back to proximity, hustle, or luck instead of intelligence. He opens three ways: institutional anointing ("The New York Post just called us…"), declarative contrarianism ("I'm giving a Rolex to the next person I hire"), or insider-dismissal ("You've probably never heard of this guy"). He moves via narrative pivots — "out of nowhere," "fast forward three years" — never logical connectors. Fragments carry payoffs. Long sentences carry context. He writes for young operators who want NYC startup-insider identity, and secondarily for CEO prospects reading the voice and thinking I want my posts to sound like this. Unbreakable rule: conventional wisdom gets named before it gets inverted.
